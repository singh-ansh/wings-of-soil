Rising Pressure

Weeks passed, and Aarav was becoming more confident in his abilities.
But with confidence came greater expectations.
The instructors now started giving more complex problems and longer sessions that tested endurance as much as intelligence.

One morning, the head instructor announced a surprise group challenge.
Students were to be divided into teams and solve a series of problems that required collaboration, quick thinking, and creativity.
Aarav felt a surge of excitement mixed with tension. He had always relied on his individual effort, but now teamwork would be the key.

Aarav’s team included students from different schools, each with unique strengths and weaknesses.
At first, conflicts arose over methods and ideas.
Some students wanted to rush, others were too cautious.
Aarav remembered Kavya’s advice about patience and listening, and he stepped in to guide his team calmly.

“Let’s try each approach one by one and see what works,” he suggested.
Slowly, the team began to synchronize.
Aarav’s confidence grew as he realized that leadership wasn’t about giving orders—it was about encouraging everyone to contribute their best.

The challenge lasted for hours.
Problems tested logical reasoning, creative thinking, and practical skills.
At one point, Aarav’s team faced a problem they couldn’t solve immediately.
He suggested taking a short break and returning with fresh minds.
When they came back, a new approach emerged, and the team solved the problem successfully.

By the end of the day, Aarav’s team had performed impressively.
Though some mistakes were made, the instructors praised their coordination, leadership, and problem-solving under pressure.
Aarav felt a deep sense of satisfaction—he had learned that strength lay not just in knowledge, but also in patience, teamwork, and resilience.

That night, Aarav sat under the starry sky with Kavya.
“The pressure is rising,” she said, smiling.
“But I know you can handle anything now.”

Aarav nodded, feeling a spark of excitement.
Every challenge, every test, was shaping him into someone stronger, ready to face the next big hurdle in life.
