The Waiting Game

After returning from the state-level scholarship exam, Aarav felt a mix of relief and anxious anticipation.
The exam was over, but the results were still weeks away.
Every day seemed longer than the last, and every evening he found himself staring at the horizon, lost in thought.

Kavya, as always, was by his side.
She encouraged him to stay patient and not dwell on what he could not control.
“Sometimes, the hardest part isn’t the challenge itself,” she said,
“it’s waiting to see the results of your effort.”

Aarav tried to follow her advice.
He spent his days revising other subjects, helping younger students at school, and practicing problem-solving.
Yet, in quiet moments, doubt would creep in.
“What if all this effort wasn’t enough?” he wondered.
“What if I fail again?”

One afternoon, Shyamlal Sir noticed Aarav’s restlessness.
He called him aside and said gently, “Aarav, the results will come in their own time.
You cannot rush them. What you can do is prepare for whatever comes next.”

Those words settled in Aarav’s mind.
He realized that worrying would not change the outcome.
All he could do was focus on his growth, on learning more, and becoming stronger.

Days turned into a week, and then another.
Aarav and Kavya continued their routine, studying together under the banyan tree, laughing at small mistakes, and celebrating little victories.
Their bond grew deeper, not just as friends but as companions sharing a journey of perseverance and hope.

Finally, the day came when the results were to be announced.
Aarav felt his heart pound with a mixture of fear and excitement.
Kavya held his hand and whispered, “No matter what happens, I’m proud of you.”

As Aarav approached the notice board, he took a deep breath.
The entire village seemed to hold its breath with him.
He scanned the list, his eyes searching for his name.
And then he saw it — Aarav Kumar, selected for the state scholarship program.
Relief, joy, and pride surged through him all at once.

He turned to Kavya, his eyes shining.
“We did it,” he said softly.
“We really did it.”

And in that moment, Aarav knew that hard work, determination, and belief in oneself were the keys to overcoming any challenge.
